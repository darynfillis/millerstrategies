Privacy Policy

How Miller Strategies handles information submitted through this website.

Effective date: April 30, 2026

Overview

Miller Strategies, LLC (“Miller Strategies,” “we,” “our,” or “us”) respects privacy and is committed to providing clear information about how we collect, use, disclose, and protect information submitted through this website.

This Privacy Policy applies to information collected through this website and related online forms. It does not apply to information collected offline, through client engagements, or through third-party websites that may be linked from this website.

Information We Collect

We may collect information that you voluntarily provide, including your name, company name, title, email address, phone number, and any message or information you submit through a contact form or email link.

We may also collect limited technical information automatically, such as browser type, device type, pages viewed, referring pages, general location inferred from IP address, and other usage data. This information may be collected through standard hosting logs, analytics tools, cookies, pixels, or similar technologies if enabled on the website.

How We Use Information

We may use information to respond to inquiries, evaluate potential client needs, communicate with you, maintain internal business records, operate and improve the website, protect the security of the website, comply with legal obligations, and support ordinary business operations.

Cookies and Analytics

This website may use cookies or similar technologies to support functionality, understand website usage, and improve performance. You can usually set your browser to block or delete cookies, but some website functionality may not work properly if cookies are disabled.

How We Share Information

We do not sell personal information submitted through this website. We may share information with internal personnel, advisors, vendors, service providers, hosting providers, analytics providers, or other parties when reasonably necessary to operate the website, respond to your inquiry, protect our rights, or comply with legal obligations.

Professional Relationship

Submitting information through this website does not create a client, legal, lobbying, advisory, fiduciary, or confidential relationship with Miller Strategies. A formal relationship is created only through an executed engagement agreement or other written agreement.

Data Security

We use reasonable administrative, technical, and organizational measures intended to protect information submitted through this website. No website, email, hosting platform, or internet transmission can be guaranteed to be fully secure.

Data Retention

We retain information for as long as reasonably necessary for the purposes described in this Privacy Policy, including to respond to inquiries, maintain records, comply with legal obligations, resolve disputes, and enforce agreements.

Your Choices

You may contact us to request that we update or delete information you submitted through the website, subject to legal, contractual, operational, or recordkeeping requirements. You may also opt out of non-essential communications by contacting us.

State Privacy Rights

Depending on your location and applicable law, you may have rights to know, access, correct, delete, or limit certain uses of personal information. We will review privacy requests in accordance with applicable law.

Children’s Privacy

This website is intended for business and professional audiences and is not directed to children under 13. We do not knowingly collect personal information from children under 13 through this website.

Third-Party Links

This website may link to third-party websites, publications, maps, or services. We are not responsible for the privacy practices, security, or content of third-party websites.

Updates to This Policy

We may update this Privacy Policy from time to time. The updated version will be posted on this page with a revised effective date.
